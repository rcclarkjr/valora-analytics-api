SKILL MASTERY INDEX (SMI) EVALUATION

Your task is to evaluate the provided artwork and return two things: an integer level and a decimal refinement. Together they form the final SMI score.

---

PART 1: DETERMINE THE INTEGER

Evaluate the artwork and assign an integer from 1 to 5 using these level descriptions. Evaluate with complete objectivity — score THIS WORK ONLY, not the artist's overall capability or potential. Judge solely on observable qualities present in this specific piece.

---

LEVEL 1 — NOVICE

Observable markers:
- Significant struggle with basic tool control; marks are wobbly, inconsistent, or unintentional
- Proportions and perspective have major errors that are clearly visible
- Color use is local and literal — colors taken directly from the subject without mixing, temperature awareness, or expressive intent
- Composition is accidental or unconsidered; elements are placed without spatial planning
- Surface is uneven with areas of overworking, muddy color, or unresolved passages
- Expressive voice is absent; work appears imitative or experimental without clear intent

Key differentiator: The gap between what the artist intended and what appears on the surface is large and visible. Execution cannot yet reliably serve vision.

---

LEVEL 2 — APPRENTICE

Observable markers:
- Basic tool control is present; marks are more intentional, though inconsistencies remain
- Proportions and perspective are mostly workable; errors are minor or localized
- Color use shows some understanding of mixing, harmony, or temperature — moving beyond purely literal color
- Composition applies learned rules (rule of thirds, symmetry, basic focal point) — formulaic but functional
- Surface is more consistent; medium is handled with growing competence
- Some personality or preference may be visible, but work is still primarily imitative or rule-following

Key differentiator: The artist can execute a plan with reasonable success. Technical problems no longer dominate the viewer's experience, but creative decisions are still governed by rules rather than personal vision.

---

LEVEL 3 — JOURNEYMAN

Observable markers:
- Confident technical execution; technique is reliable and can be adapted to serve creative needs
- Compositions go beyond formulas; uses negative space, rhythm, asymmetry, or other devices with intention
- Color choices are personal and confident; may push saturation, use limited palettes, or employ temperature expressively
- Surface handling is refined; mark-making is consistent and beginning to suggest a recognizable hand
- Work feels authored rather than anonymous; small seeds of personal voice are sprouting
- Can attempt challenging effects and largely succeed

Key differentiator: Technical mastery is established. The work is clearly professional in quality. Personal voice is emerging but not yet consistent or fully committed.

---

LEVEL 4 — EXPERT

Observable markers:
- Masterful technical execution that serves creative intent completely; technique is a tool, never a limitation
- Can break technical rules successfully and intentionally for expressive effect
- Sophisticated, distinctive compositional voice — compositions feel fresh, guide the viewer intentionally, and are recognizable as belonging to this artist
- Distinctive color sensibility — palette choices feel personal, intentional, and expressive; color identity is forming
- Recognizable surface quality and mark-making character; consistent stylistic choices in texture or finish
- Clear personal aesthetic preferences visible across the work; work has personality, point of view, and is beginning to be recognizable as by this specific creator

Key differentiator: The work has a system. Color, composition, surface, and voice reinforce each other as a unified aesthetic. The artist is not just making good individual choices — they are expressing a consistent vision.

---

LEVEL 5 — MASTER

Observable markers:
- Technique becomes invisible; the viewer responds to emotion, meaning, or beauty first — execution is so assured it disappears
- Economy and restraint: maximum impact with minimum elements; nothing excessive, nothing missing; what is excluded is as important as what is included
- Inevitable rightness: every choice feels necessary; cannot imagine the work any other way; appears effortless despite profound sophistication
- Timeless quality: transcends stylistic trends; feels complete and universal rather than tied to a particular aesthetic movement
- Work operates beyond style and technique into the realm of profound expression
- Unreproducible by others despite apparent simplicity; emotional or conceptual depth resonates universally

Key differentiator: Work achieves depth that transcends personal style into universal territory. Technique has fully dissolved into vision.

---

YOUR INTEGER IS NOW LOCKED.

---

PART 2: DETERMINE THE DECIMAL

The SMI score is the sum of two parts: the integer and a decimal. Your only task in Part 2 is to determine the decimal portion.

Examine the artwork carefully and answer each of the following 6 questions Yes or No. In edge cases, select the answer that comes closest to being true.

1. Is the mark-making consistent enough across the work to suggest a recognizable hand rather than varied or inconsistent handling?

2. Does the work establish an identifiable emotional atmosphere or mood that feels intentional rather than neutral or accidental?

3. Does the quality of edges — hard or soft — appropriately support the work's intent?

4. Are value relationships structured to serve the work's focal point rather than simply recording observed light and shadow accurately?

5. Does the entire surface area feel resolved and intentional, save any areas of deliberate simplicity or economy?

6. Does the color palette demonstrate personal authorship — departing from purely conventional or descriptive color in at least one meaningful way — while remaining intentional and designed to enhance the mood or narrative of the painting?

Count your Yes answers and use the table below to identify the decimal portion of the SMI score:
