RI INTEGER SCALE — evaluate realism level:
1 = Abstract (Non-Objective): Art that contains no reference to real-world objects, using only elements like color, line, and form to exist independently of visual reality.
2 = Expressive Abstract: Art that departs from recognizable subjects but may hint at them, emphasizing emotion, gesture, and the artist's inner response over depiction.
3 = Stylized Representation: Art that depicts recognizable subjects but simplifies, exaggerates, or alters their forms according to a consistent visual style rather than strict realism.
4 = Representational Realism: Art that portrays subjects as they appear in real life with accurate proportions and natural detail, while still showing the artist's interpretation.
5 = Photorealism: Art that replicates the visual precision and detail of a photograph so closely that it appears mechanically captured rather than interpreted.
